Understanding the Probate Process in Friendswood

Before you can sell an inherited property in Texas, you typically need to complete probate proceedings in Galveston County. This legal process validates the will and transfers ownership rights. Depending on whether the estate has a will and how it was structured, probate can take several months to over a year. Working with a probate attorney familiar with Texas law helps ensure you have clear title before listing or accepting offers. Some properties may qualify for simplified probate procedures if the estate value falls below certain thresholds.

Common Challenges When Selling Inherited Homes

Many inherited properties in Friendswood neighborhoods like Countryside or Wedgewood require significant repairs or updates. The previous owner may have lived there for decades, leaving outdated kitchens, old HVAC systems, or deferred maintenance issues. Traditional buyers often request extensive repairs or offer lower prices after inspections. Additionally, if multiple heirs inherited the property, reaching agreement on listing price, repairs, and timing can create family conflicts. Property taxes and maintenance costs continue accumulating while you decide what to do, adding financial pressure to an emotional situation.
