The Legal Side: Selling Your House Fast in Texas

Texas real estate transactions have some unique characteristics you should understand:

Promulgated Contracts

Texas uses standardized contracts promulgated by the Texas Real Estate Commission. When working with Jason The House Buyer (License #0657354), you can trust you're working with a licensed professional who follows all Texas real estate regulations.

Title and Closing

In Texas, title companies handle closings rather than attorneys (unlike in many other states). We work with reputable Houston-area title companies to ensure smooth, secure transactions. You'll receive your proceeds via wire transfer or cashier's check at closing.

Seller Disclosures

Texas law requires sellers to complete a Seller's Disclosure Notice about the property's condition. Even when selling as-is for cash, you're still obligated to disclose known material defects. However, cash buyers are much more willing to accept properties with disclosed issues than traditional buyers.

Property Taxes

Texas property taxes are relatively high compared to other states since we have no state income tax. If you're behind on property taxes, this doesn't prevent a cash sale. Property taxes are typically prorated at closing, and any liens are paid from the proceeds.
