The Traditional Route: Why It's Harder for Rental Properties

Selling a rental property the conventional way in Humble comes with unique obstacles. First, if you have tenants in place, scheduling showings becomes a nightmare. Texas law requires you to give proper notice to tenants, but that doesn't mean they'll cooperate or keep the property show-ready.

Second, most retail buyers looking in Humble want move-in ready homes. They're shopping for properties near good schools like Humble ISD or in newer developments like Timber Creek or The Groves. If your rental has deferred maintenance, outdated finishes, or tenant-caused damage, you'll face either costly repairs or significant price reductions.

Finally, if you need to evict tenants before selling, you're looking at months of legal proceedings in Harris County courts, plus attorney fees that can easily exceed $3,000-$5,000.

Understanding Your Numbers: What Landlords Need to Know

Before you sell your Humble rental property, it's important to understand the financial implications. As a Texas landlord, you'll want to consider:

Capital gains taxes: If your property has appreciated (and with Humble's growth, it probably has), you may owe taxes on the gain. Talk with a CPA about 1031 exchanges if you're considering buying another investment property.

Depreciation recapture: The IRS will want back some of those depreciation deductions you've been taking. Again, a tax professional can help you understand your specific situation.

Final accounting with tenants: Under Texas Property Code, you'll need to properly handle security deposits and provide required documentation, even when selling to a cash buyer.
