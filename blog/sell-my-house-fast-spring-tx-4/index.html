What Does Selling As-Is Really Mean in Spring, Texas?

When you sell a home as-is in Texas, you're offering the property in its current condition with no obligation to make repairs or improvements before closing. This is a legally recognized transaction type under Texas real estate law, and it's particularly common in the Houston metro area where many homes face issues like foundation problems, outdated systems, or damage from Houston's notorious weather events.

However, it's important to understand that selling as-is doesn't mean you can hide known defects. Under Texas Property Code, sellers must still complete a Seller's Disclosure Notice and honestly report any known material defects. The difference is that buyers acknowledge they're purchasing the home in its current state and won't request repairs as a condition of sale.
