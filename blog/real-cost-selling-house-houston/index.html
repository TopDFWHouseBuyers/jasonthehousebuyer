The Real Cost of Selling a House in Houston - Fees and Hidden Costs

Published June 06, 2026 · Houston, Texas
Back to All Articles

Selling your Houston home seems straightforward until you start calculating what you will actually walk away with after the sale. Between realtor commissions, closing costs, repairs, and surprise expenses, the cost of selling a house in Houston typically ranges from 10-15% of your home's sale price—sometimes even more.

Realtor Commissions: The Biggest Expense

In Houston, realtor commissions typically consume 5-6% of your home's sale price. On a $300,000 home, that amounts to $15,000-$18,000 split between the listing agent and buyer's agent. While this percentage can be negotiated, most Houston real estate transactions follow this standard rate. This single expense often shocks sellers who did not factor it into their net proceeds calculations.

Seller Closing Costs in Houston

Beyond commissions, Houston sellers face additional closing costs totaling 1-3% of the sale price. These include the title policy (which Texas tradition dictates the seller pays), escrow fees, transfer taxes, and attorney fees. In Harris County and surrounding areas like Katy, Sugar Land, and The Woodlands, you will also need to provide a survey if one does not exist. Prorated property taxes, homeowner association dues, and outstanding liens must be settled at closing as well.

Pre-Sale Repairs and Improvements

Most Houston homes require some preparation before listing. Fresh paint, landscaping, deep cleaning, and minor repairs can easily cost $3,000-$10,000. If your home inspection reveals issues—common in our humid climate like foundation concerns, roof damage, or HVAC problems—buyers will request repairs or credits. Many Cypress, Tomball, and Humble homeowners spend $5,000-$20,000 addressing inspection items just to keep deals from falling apart.

Hidden Costs That Add Up

Several expenses catch Houston sellers off guard. Staging services run $1,500-$3,000 monthly. Professional photography costs $200-$500. If you need to move before closing, you will carry two mortgages plus utilities. Homeowners in Pearland, League City, and Friendswood often forget about HOA fees, lawn maintenance during the listing period, and potential price reductions if the home sits on the market. Each month your house does not sell costs you mortgage payments, insurance, utilities, and maintenance—typically $2,000-$4,000 monthly.

Capital Gains Taxes

While many homeowners qualify for capital gains exclusions, those who have not lived in their Houston home for two of the last five years may owe taxes on profits. Investment properties and second homes face significant tax implications that reduce your net proceeds substantially.

The Timeline Factor

Traditional Houston home sales take 60-90 days on average, but many properties in Pasadena, Baytown, and Conroe sit longer. Every additional month means continued mortgage payments, insurance, taxes, utilities, and maintenance. These carrying costs can add thousands to your total selling expenses.
