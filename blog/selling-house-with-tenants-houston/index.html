Selling a House With Tenants in Houston: A Complete Landlord Guide

As a Houston landlord, selling a rental property while tenants are still living there presents unique challenges and legal considerations. Whether you inherited tenants, are dealing with difficult renters, or simply want to cash out your investment, understanding your options and obligations under Texas law is crucial for a smooth transaction.

Understanding Texas Tenant Rights When Selling

In Texas, landlords have specific legal obligations when selling occupied rental properties. If your tenants have an active lease, that agreement typically transfers to the new owner. Month-to-month tenants require proper notice before vacating. Houston landlords must provide reasonable notice before showings, typically 24 hours, though Texas law does not specify an exact timeframe. Violating tenant rights can lead to legal complications that delay your sale and cost money.

The biggest challenge many Houston landlords face is coordinating showings around tenant schedules. Uncooperative tenants may refuse access, keep the property in poor condition, or discourage potential buyers. These situations make traditional sales difficult and time-consuming.

Your Options for Selling a House With Tenants in Houston

You have several approaches when selling your occupied rental property in Houston. The first option is selling to an investor who wants to keep the tenants and continue collecting rent. This works well if you have good tenants with lease agreements in place, as many investors prefer turnkey rental properties.

Another option is offering cash for keys, where you negotiate with tenants to vacate early in exchange for a payment. This can expedite the sale process but adds to your costs. You could also wait until the lease expires, though this delays your sale timeline significantly.

The third option is selling the property as-is to a cash buyer who handles the tenant situation for you.
